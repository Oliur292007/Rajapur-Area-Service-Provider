Saturia Zamindar Bari:

History

Sher-e-Bangla A.K. Fazlul Haque, known as the Tiger of Bengal, was born on 26 October 1873 in the Saturia Zamindar house. [ 2 ] It is said that this Zamindar house was established in the 17th century. It was the house of Fazlul Haque's mother. Fazlul Haque spent his childhood in this house of his maternal grandfather. He used the Maktab of this house for his primary education. Apart from his childhood, he spent much of his working and political life here. Fazlul Haque's maternal grandfather Ali Mia established a zamindari in this region. It is believed that he was the one who founded this zamindar house.

Infrastructure

The entire zamindar house is built on 100 acres of land. The zamindar house is surrounded by a pond, garden and a few buildings. The main compound, built in the Mughal architectural style, consists of 3 buildings. The walls of each building have various designs in the Mughal style. There is a main entrance to enter the main compound. Bricks were also used in the construction of the buildings along with lime mortar. The ruins of the zamindar house can be seen today. Currently, several institutions, including the Sher-e-Bangla Memorial Library, have been established in this landlord's house in memory of Sher-e-Bangla. The house is currently looked after by the descendants of Ali Mia.
