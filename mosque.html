Jhalakathi's archaeological heritage: Khanbari Mosque Complex in Rajapur:

Khanbari Mosque complex is located in the village of Angaria in Rajapur upazila of Jhalakathi district. It is located 11.5 kilometers southwest of Jhalakathi district headquarters and 2.5 kilometers southeast of Rajapur upazila headquarters. This mosque complex in Angaria village is located in front of the house of former President Maulvi Asmat Ali Khan. The narrow Jangalia River flows about 700 meters north and east of this archaeological site. Two brothers named Asar Khan and Kishore Khan are the first ancestors of Maulvi Asmat Ali Khan. Around the first half of the 18th century, Asar Khan and Kishore Khan arrived in Rajapur, Jhalakathi to spread Islam.

Kishore Khan built three single-domed mosques. According to legend, the names of these three mosques are 'Allah', 'Muhammad' and 'Fatema'. Kishore Khan's tomb is located to the south of the mosques. The Khanbari Mosque complex has three mosques, ranging from small to large. These single- domed mosques are built in almost the same architectural style. There are 4 minarets at the four corners of the three mosques. There are decorated finials on the domes and minarets. The outer walls of the mosques are decorated with various designs. There is an entrance on the eastern wall of the mosques. There are decorations on the outer walls of these semicircular arched entrances. There are three steps of stairs in front of the mosques. This ancient monument of Muslim architecture is a protected monument declared by the Government of the People's Republic of Bangladesh. This archaeological heritage of Jhalakati district is listed and supervised by the Department of Archaeology.
