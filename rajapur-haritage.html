Heritage of Rajapur:

Everyone's favorite 'Shital Pati' art:

It has been passed down from generation to generation for approximately two and a half to three hundred years. This art is widespread in some specific areas of Rajapur Upazila. Rajapur was not the only place where it was famous. Many wise men were born in the traditional silvery Bengal of Jibanananda. Shital Pati is the main furniture for decorating their dream homes. A beautiful and tidy bedroom erases the fatigue of the day, a comfortable bed brings a happy sleep.

Price of the 'Shital Pati':

Price of a 5ft x 7ft 'Shital Pati': 1,000 to 1,600 taka.

Price of a 5ft x 6ft 'Shital Pati': 800 to 1,200 taka.

Price of a 5ft x 4.5ft 'Shital Pati': 800 to 1,000 taka.

Weaving Industry:

This industry was famous in various areas of Rajapur Upazila for approximately one and a half to two hundred years. The weaving industry of Rajapur Upazila, especially the gamcha, was famous throughout the country. Yarn is the main material of this industry. Therefore, due to the increase in the price of yarn and insufficient supply, the families involved in this craft are unable to meet the demand for their products due to lack of capital. The weaving families of Algi, Kaivartakhali and Angaria in Rajapur. Just 10 to 15 years ago, the women of these families used to make velvet sarees, hajar buti sarees, jhapashari, in addition to these sarees, gamcha, lungi, moshari and even chadar.

Pan Baraj:

Betel cultivation in Rajapur Upazila has a long history. An estimated 100 to 150 years old tradition is associated with this betel cultivation. Pan means tambul. The only literature of the Middle Ages is written in the poem called Sri Krishna Kirtan. The word tambul (betel) is mentioned in the Tambul section of this poem. Sri Krishna, fascinated by the form of Radha, sends betel to Radha through bragging. This proves that betel has been a bridge between men and women for ages. Even today, in rural Bengal, there is not a single pair of betel leaves as a guest-entertaining item. Not only that, there is a competition to decorate betel leaves in every household in Bengal during festivals. The feeling of love is expressed with a betel leaf.
