Galua Paka Mosque

History of the mosque

It is said that a religious man named Mahmud Khan Akan (Mamuji) built this mosque. It is believed that it was built in Bengali in 1122. An inscription was found near the mosque and from it the date of its construction is estimated. There is a popular story that when the bushes were being cleared to help in the renovation of this mosque, large poisonous snakes came out of it. Later, when a part was opened as per the advice of a local Huzoor, the snakes left the place. [ 1 ] This mosque is located one kilometer east of Galua Bazar, adjacent to Barabari. The mosque was built about three hundred years ago. It was built in 1122 by a local influential devout Muslim named Mahmudjan Akand. The date of its construction is found in an abandoned rock quarry. Its wings are quite wide. This mosque is made of tiled bricks. As the mosque has been demolished, its bricks are not visible at present.

On the eve of independence, when the mosque was on the verge of collapse, it was renovated by the efforts of a local philanthropist , F.M.A. Quddus Khan. The veranda in front of the mosque has been extended. In 1999, the Bangladesh government brought this mosque under the control of the Department of Archaeology. Therefore, the government allocated funds for the renovation of the mosque.
